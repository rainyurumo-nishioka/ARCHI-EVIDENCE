## Purpose

ARCHI is a design for coexistence.  
But coexistence requires understanding why the current structure fails.

This repository documents that failure — and points toward alternatives.

**Three questions guide this work:**

1. How did the current political and social structure come to be?
2. Where is it failing — and why?
3. What would a different structure look like?

---

## Why Japan First

Japan is the starting point because:

- It is where this project lives
- Its postwar structure was largely designed by an external power — making the architecture unusually visible
- Its current dysfunctions (political corruption, opaque foreign policy decisions, demographic policy contradictions) are traceable to that original design

Understanding Japan requires understanding the occupation period (1945–1952) and the documents that shaped it.

## Method

This is not journalism. This is not political commentary.

This is **structural analysis** — the same way an engineer reads a blueprint.

For each topic, the analysis follows this format:

**1. What the record shows**  
Direct reference to source documents. Facts only.

**2. What structure this reveals**  
Who held decision-making power. Who benefited. What was hidden.

**3. Where the structure is failing now**  
Current dysfunction traced back to structural origin.

**4. What a different structure could look like**  
Not utopia. Minimum viable alternatives — drawn from ARCHI principles.

---

## Principles

- **Facts only in the record.** No speculation stored as fact.
- **Sources cited for every claim.** No unsourced assertions.
- **Structure, not individuals.** The target is the system, not the person.
- **Open to correction.** If a record is wrong, it can be updated with evidence.
- **Permanent.** Once verified, records do not disappear.

## Starting Point

**Japan, occupation period, 1945–1952.**

Key questions:
- What decisions were made during this period that still shape Japan's political structure today?
- Which of those decisions were made by Japanese actors, and which were imposed or negotiated?
- What do US declassified documents reveal that Japanese public records do not?

This is where the research begins.
